Tax-Law

Service-Tax

The government levies a service tax on all services provided by businesses and service providers in exchange for monetary compensation. The service tax levied on services is actually borne by the customers, which in turn goes through multiple channels of levying authorities until it gets back to the government. It should also be noted that service tax will only apply to taxable services provided or to be provided by a service provider who has agreed to the concern of actually offering services.

Income-Tax

Income tax is a tax levied on people or entities (taxpayers) based on their income or profits (taxable income). In general, income tax is calculated as the product of a tax rate multiplied by taxable revenue. The income tax received by the Income Tax Department is for the benefit of the central government.

Customs_duty-Tax

Customs duty is a tax on the import and export of products in India, with different rates applied to different kinds of goods. Customs officials have the right to inspect accurate details of items exported or imported, as well as the origin of the item and properly validated rates and structures. Customs duty assesses the value of an item in the context of the tax applied to that item and is much higher on certain items, such as sin products such as liquor and imported cigars.

Property-tax

Property tax, also known as "house tax," is a local tax levied on owners of buildings and appurtenant land (certainly not true custodians of property as per the 1978 44th Amendment to the Constitution). It is similar to a wealth tax in the United States but varies from an excise tax in the United Kingdom. The taxing authority is vested in the states, and it is delegated to local bodies by legislation, which specifies the valuation technique, rate band, and collection procedures. The annual rental value (ARV) or area-based rating serves as the tax basis.
