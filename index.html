Property Law

The area of law that governs what individuals own is known as property law. It is the branch of law that governs who can and cannot own land and personal property, as well as how and under what circumstances they can use it. Real property and personal property are both covered by property law. Property ownership and use is an area of legislation that affects everyone in society.

Common Law

The Common Law is a body of law that is drawn from judicial decisions known as case laws rather than statutes. From time immemorial, the Common Law drew its authority from the universal consent and practise of the people. This jurisprudential system started in England. Common law is incomprehensible until it is stated in a judgement. It encompasses the legal rules that draw their authority from the statements of principles found in court decisions. This legal system incorporates tradition, custom, and usage as well as basic principles and modes of reasoning. It is the embodiment of wide and comprehensive unwritten principles drawn from natural reasoning and an innate sense of justice.
