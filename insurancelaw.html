Insurance-Law

1

The field of law pertaining to insurance, including insurance policies and claims, is known as insurance law. Generally speaking, it can be divided into three categories: regulation of the insurance industry; regulation of insurance policy language, particularly with respect to consumer policies; and regulation of claim handling procedures.

2

The insurance sector underwent a full cycle of phases, beginning with total deregulation and ending with comprehensive regulation. A variety of acts regulate it. The Indian Life Insurance Companies Act, 1912, was the first law to govern the life insurance industry in India. The Insurance Act of 1938[7] was the first piece of law to strictly impose state control over the insurance industry and cover all types of insurance. On January 19, 1956, the Life Insurance Corporation Act fully nationalized life insurance in India. The Life Insurance Corporation of India was formed following the merger of all 245 insurance firms that were then doing business in the nation.

3

In order to nationalise the approximately 100 general insurance firms that existed at the time and combine them into four companies, the General Insurance Business Act of 1972 was created. All the businesses were combined to form National Insurance, New India Assurance, Oriental Insurance, and United India Insurance, with offices in each of the four major towns. There were no private insurance firms in India prior to 1999. Then, in 1999, the government passed the Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority Act, deregulating the insurance industry and allowing for private businesses. Additionally, foreign investment was permitted with a limit of 26% ownership in Indian insurance firms. The cap on foreign direct investment in the insurance industry was raised to 49 percent in 2015, subject to a few restrictions.

4

In order to give the profession statutory standing on par with chartered accountants, notaries, cost and works accountants, advocates, architects, and company secretaries, the Actuaries Act was enacted by parliament in 2006. A minimum capital of US$80 million (roughly Rs 400 crore) is needed to establish an insurance company, according to the law.
